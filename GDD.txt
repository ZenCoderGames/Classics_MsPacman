================================================================================
GAME DESIGN DOCUMENT
Ms. Pac-Man (Faithful Recreation)
================================================================================

Version:   1.0
Status:    Draft
Genre:     Arcade / Maze Chase
Platform:  Desktop (Windows / macOS / Linux)
Players:   1 (alternating 2-player supported)
Audience:  All ages; fans of classic arcade games


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. HIGH-LEVEL CONCEPT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Ms. Pac-Man is a top-down maze chase game. The player guides Ms. Pac-Man
through an enclosed maze, eating every pellet while avoiding four colored
ghosts. Eating a large Power Pellet briefly turns the ghosts vulnerable,
letting Ms. Pac-Man eat them for bonus points. Clearing all pellets in a maze
advances the player to the next level.

The game is a faithful recreation of the 1982 arcade classic, emphasizing tight
controls, deterministic-yet-varied ghost behavior, four rotating mazes, moving
bonus fruit, and escalating difficulty.

Design Pillars:
  - Readable, fair, twitch-precise movement.
  - Distinct ghost personalities that create emergent tension.
  - Risk/reward loop around Power Pellets and bonus fruit.
  - "One more try" replayability through score chasing.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. CORE GAMEPLAY LOOP
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  1. Navigate the maze and eat pellets (dots).
  2. Avoid ghosts; being touched by a non-frightened ghost costs a life.
  3. Eat Power Pellets to make ghosts frightened, then hunt them for points.
  4. Grab moving bonus fruit for extra points.
  5. Eat all pellets to clear the maze and advance to the next level.
  6. Repeat with faster ghosts, shorter frightened durations, and new mazes.
  7. Lose all lives -> Game Over -> score recorded to high score table.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. CONTROLS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Movement is 4-directional (up, down, left, right) along the maze grid.
Input is "buffered": pressing a direction turns Ms. Pac-Man as soon as the
next tile allows it, enabling smooth cornering.

  Keyboard (primary):
    - Arrow Keys ........ Move Up / Down / Left / Right
    - W A S D ........... Alternate movement
    - P or Esc .......... Pause / Resume
    - Enter / Space ..... Confirm / Start
    - M ................. Mute / Unmute

  Gamepad (optional):
    - D-Pad / Left Stick  Move
    - Start ............. Pause
    - A / Cross ......... Confirm


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
4. THE MAZE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

- The playfield is a tile-based grid (28 tiles wide x 31 tiles tall,
  classic proportions).
- Walls block movement; corridors are one tile wide.
- One or two horizontal "warp tunnels" connect the left and right edges;
  entities exiting one side reappear on the other. Ghosts move at reduced
  speed while inside tunnels.
- The ghost house (pen) sits at the maze center; ghosts start or respawn here.
- Four distinct maze layouts rotate as the player progresses (see Section 11).
- Maze color changes per level group for visual variety.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
5. COLLECTIBLES & SCORING
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Pellets (Dots):
  - Small pellet ............ 10 points
  - Power Pellet (energizer)  50 points
  - 4 Power Pellets per maze (one near each corner region).

Ghosts (while frightened):
  - 1st ghost eaten ......... 200 points
  - 2nd ghost eaten ......... 400 points
  - 3rd ghost eaten ......... 800 points
  - 4th ghost eaten ......... 1600 points
  - Multiplier resets when the Power Pellet effect ends.

Bonus Fruit (moving; enters via a tunnel and roams the maze):
  - Cherry .................. 100 points
  - Strawberry ............. 200 points
  - Orange ................. 500 points
  - Pretzel ................ 700 points
  - Apple .................. 1000 points
  - Pear .................. 2000 points
  - Banana ................ 5000 points
  - Fruit appears twice per maze after a set number of pellets are eaten,
    then leaves if not collected in time.

Extra Life:
  - Awarded at 10,000 points (one time). Optional setting for additional.

Level Clear:
  - No direct points; advances the level and increments difficulty.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
6. THE GHOSTS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Four ghosts pursue Ms. Pac-Man, each with a signature targeting behavior.
Ghosts cycle between three global modes on a timer: SCATTER (retreat to a home
corner), CHASE (pursue using personality logic), and FRIGHTENED (flee, edible).

  Blinky (Red) .... Aggressive. Chase target = Ms. Pac-Man's current tile.
                    Speeds up ("Cruise Elroy") as pellets dwindle.

  Pinky (Pink) .... Ambusher. Chase target = a few tiles ahead of
                    Ms. Pac-Man's facing direction.

  Inky (Cyan) ..... Unpredictable. Chase target derived from both Blinky's
                    position and Ms. Pac-Man's, producing flanking behavior.

  Sue (Orange) .... Shy. Chases when far away; scatters to its corner when
                    close to Ms. Pac-Man.

Ghost States:
  - Normal:      Uses scatter/chase target. Contact = lose a life.
  - Frightened:  Turns blue, slows, moves semi-randomly, flees.
                 Contact = ghost eaten (points), reverts to eyes.
  - Eaten (eyes): Fast-travels back to the ghost house, then respawns.

Note: In Ms. Pac-Man, ghost movement includes intentional randomness,
making patterns less exploitable than the original Pac-Man.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
7. POWER PELLET / FRIGHTENED MECHANIC
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

- Eating a Power Pellet sets all active ghosts to FRIGHTENED.
- Frightened ghosts reverse direction, slow down, and become edible.
- A visual "warning flash" signals the last ~2 seconds before the effect ends.
- Frightened duration shrinks as levels increase; at high levels it may reach
  zero (ghosts no longer turn blue).
- Eating consecutive ghosts within one Power Pellet chains the score
  multiplier (200 -> 400 -> 800 -> 1600).


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
8. LIVES, DEATH & GAME OVER
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

- The player starts with 3 lives (configurable: 1-5).
- Touching a non-frightened ghost triggers a death animation and costs a life.
- The maze state (eaten pellets) persists across deaths within a level.
- After death, Ms. Pac-Man and ghosts reset to starting positions.
- When lives reach zero, the game ends and the score is submitted to the
  local high score table.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
9. DIFFICULTY PROGRESSION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

As levels increase:
  - Ghost base speed increases.
  - Frightened duration decreases (down to none).
  - Cruise Elroy (Blinky speed-up) triggers earlier.
  - Bonus fruit values increase.
  - Maze layout rotates on a fixed schedule.

Speed is capped so the game remains fair but demanding at high levels.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
10. GAME STATES / SCREENS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  - Attract / Title Screen: Logo, "Press Start", high score, demo/attract mode.
  - Ready State: "READY!" prompt before play begins in a maze.
  - Playing: Active gameplay.
  - Paused: Overlay; gameplay frozen.
  - Life Lost: Death animation, then reset or Game Over.
  - Level Clear / Intermission: Maze flash, occasional cut-scene between
    level groups (classic act intermissions).
  - Game Over: Final score, high score entry if qualified.
  - High Score Table: Top scores with initials.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
11. LEVEL / MAZE ROTATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Four mazes rotate across levels (classic schedule):
  - Maze A (Pink) ...... Levels 1-2
  - Maze B (Light Blue)  Levels 3-5
  - Maze C (Brown) ..... Levels 6-9
  - Maze D (Dark Blue) . Levels 10-13
  - Then the C/D cycle repeats for remaining levels.

Intermission cut-scenes ("Acts") play between certain level groups to tell the
lighthearted story of Pac-Man and Ms. Pac-Man.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
12. AUDIO
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Music:
  - Attract/title jingle.
  - Level start "READY!" tune.
  - Intermission cut-scene music.
  - Game Over sting.

Sound Effects:
  - Pellet-eating "waka" loop.
  - Power Pellet siren shift.
  - Ghost eaten.
  - Fruit eaten.
  - Death sound.
  - Extra life chime.
  - Ghost "eyes returning" hum.

Ambient siren tempo rises as fewer pellets remain, increasing tension.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
13. ART & VISUAL STYLE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

- Faithful pixel-art aesthetic evoking the 1982 arcade cabinet.
- Bright, high-contrast palette; each ghost has a unique, readable color.
- Ms. Pac-Man distinguished by her red bow, lipstick, and beauty mark.
- Crisp sprite animation: mouth chomp, ghost wobble, frightened blue/white,
  ghost eyes.
- Clean HUD: score, high score, remaining lives (Ms. Pac-Man icons), and
  collected fruit row.
- Optional CRT scanline / bloom filter toggle for authenticity.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
16. WIN / LOSE CONDITIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  Advance:  Eat every pellet and Power Pellet in the current maze.
  Lose life: Contact a non-frightened ghost.
  Game Over: All lives lost.
  Goal:     Maximize score; the game is endless until Game Over
            (classic kill-screen behavior at extreme levels is out of scope).


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
17. ACCESSIBILITY & OPTIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  - Remappable controls.
  - Adjustable starting lives and extra-life threshold.
  - Master / music / SFX volume sliders; mute toggle.
  - Colorblind-friendly ghost indicators (optional glyphs).
  - Toggle CRT filter.
  - Optional slow / practice mode for new players.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
18. STRETCH GOALS (NON-CORE)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  - Online / local high-score leaderboard.
  - Additional custom mazes and a maze editor.
  - Cooperative or versus multiplayer modes.
  - Achievements and daily challenge seeds.
  - Full recreation of all classic intermission cut-scenes.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
19. GLOSSARY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  Pellet / Dot ...... Small collectible worth 10 points.
  Power Pellet ...... Large energizer; frightens ghosts.
  Frightened ........ Ghost state in which ghosts are edible.
  Scatter ........... Ghost mode; retreat to a home corner.
  Chase ............. Ghost mode; actively pursue using personality logic.
  Cruise Elroy ...... Blinky's late-maze speed-up behavior.
  Warp Tunnel ....... Edge passage linking left and right sides of the maze.
  Ghost House / Pen . Central enclosure where ghosts start and respawn.
  Intermission ...... Non-interactive story cut-scene between level groups.
